SIGIRIYA


Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress, which is also known as Lion Rock. It is located in Central Sri Lanka. It was built by King Kashyapa during the reign of 473-495 which stand majestically 660 feet straight up.



Before it was made by the Sinhalese king, the area was a large forest. After storms and landslides it became a hill and was selected by King Kashyapa for his new capital. He built his palace on top the sigiriya rock and filled the sides with beautiful frescoes.



Sigiriya is one of Sri Lanka's most important historical sites. Locals refer to this ancient palace and fortress complex as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," and it attracts thousands of tourists each year. It is undoubtedly Sri Lanka's most popular tourist attraction.
